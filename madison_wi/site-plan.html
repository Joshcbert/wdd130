Content

Home page

Come to Madison Wisconsin! There are many fun things to do and places to see around town. Madison is the state capital and is built on an isthmus between Lake Mendota and Lake Monona. Only one other city is built on an isthmus (Seattle, WA). It has a diverse population with young professionals, government employees and students attending the University of Wisconsin Madison. Go Badgers! Enjoy fun for the whole family in a unique and beautiful city. The best part is you can get out of the city in less than 30 minutes and feel like you are out on the farm. The best of both worlds within minutes. Determine who has the best cheese curds between The Great Dane, The Old Fashioned or Dotty Dumpling's Dowry. Or go to Ian’s Pizza and try one of their crazy pizza flavors like mac and Cheese, baked penne or Drunken Ravioli.
Did you also know that Madison is one of the top ranked cities for bikeability! People love to be outside biking or hiking around out.

Downtown

Olbrich Gardens - Broken up into zone you can see a diverse spread of beautiful flowers and other plants. And don’t forget to stop by the hidden Royal Thai Pavillion!
State Street and the Capitol Building - In the heart of downtown you can see the capitol building. This unique building has the only granite dome in the US and is a beautiful building to tour. You can try and find the 6 fossils in the tile and stone used to build the build or in the summer go up and walk the perimeter of the dome getting a breathtaking view of Madison from all angles. Also did you know that it is illegal to have a building taller than the capital build nearby? It allows for the building to be seen from all around and creates some great pictures from the lakes. After walking the capitol building stroll down State street, a pedestrian only road with lots of fun local shops with lots of character.
Farmer’s Market - Through the summer and fall Madison has the largest producer only farmer’s market every saturday around the capitol building. Come enjoy some great fresh produce or a sweet treat from one of the many local vendors . Yum!
Children’s Museum - Take the kids to the children’s museum and spark their sense of wonder. With 3D puzzles, an art studio and lots things to climb on or interact with you won’t be able to knock the smile off your kid’s face!
Henry Villas Zoo - Visit Henry Vilas Zoo! This zoo is free to come and visit as part of the conditions set when the Vilas donated the land to the city of Madison in honor of their son who died at 27 years old to to health complications.
Monona Trail - Enjoy the beautiful Monona trail. This paved path circles around Lake Monona. It is great for biking, walking or running. Stop at one of the many parks off the trail and play some volleyball too. Or watch the sail boats out on the water. A perfect place to get a picturesque view.

Around Town

Describe the many things to do that are around Madison but not in Madison. Ice Age Trails - Hike along one of the many beautiful segments of the Ice Age trail. This trail follows where the glaciers stopped back during the ice age. In fact it is due to the glaciers that Wisconsin is as flat as it is. When the glaciers melted it smashed and leveled all the land around it.
Devils Lake State Park - This is everyone’s favorite state park to visit. Play sand volleyball, picnic on the lake shore, rent a paddleboard or take a quick hike to the Devil’s Doorway. With so many unique attractions it’s a clear winner.
Paddleboarding - Between lake Wingra, Mendota and Manona you can kayak or paddleboard all over in Madison. Better yet they will often have Full Moon paddles where there will be musicians on the lake and people can paddle out to enjoy the ambiance.
Epic - This is the largest Healthcare software company in the US and you’ll be surprised to see how they all work there. With over 30 themed buildings you can be in the wild west, a sci-fi adventure or in a stable. Who knew offices could be so fun. An absolute must see. You could spend all day on a self guided tour.
Apple Orchard - Go to schuster’s farm or Ski-Hi in the fall to pic apples and enjoy cider and apple cider donuts.
